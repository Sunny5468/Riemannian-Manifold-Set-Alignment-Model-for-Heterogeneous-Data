Addressing the task of EEG-fNIRS heterogeneous brain signal fusion, this study proposes a technical pipeline featuring intra-modal Riemannian alignment, inter-modal shared space mapping, and deep fusion classification. Driven by the significant disparities between EEG and fNIRS in sampling rates, dynamic responses, noise sources, and statistical structures—which often cause models to learn modal biases rather than task-related structures—this research formulates alignment as a geometric normalization problem on the SPD covariance manifold, utilizing Riemannian mean normalization to mitigate individual and device variances while enhancing cross-modal comparability in a shared space. Experimental results from single-subject visualizations and statistical alignment across 29 subjects demonstrate a significant reduction in paired distances and a stable increase in canonical correlation; meanwhile, a five-step ablation framework has been established, with EEG single-modality classification already verified and four remaining control groups currently under evaluation. By detailing the dataset selection, implementation workflow, and key hyperparameters, this work establishes a robust foundation for subsequent cross-subject generalization assessments and statistical significance testing.
